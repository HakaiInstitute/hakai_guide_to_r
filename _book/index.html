Preface

One of Hakai’s core themes that cross-cuts each research axis is ‘Big-Data and Modelling’. Our ability to gain insight from our long-term ecological data sets is limited only by our capacity to integrate, and digest data. To that end, this guide was developed to serve as a foundation from which to build the internal technical capacity — using a coherent and systematic approach — to turn data into insight, understanding, and ultimately knowledge about the ecosystems we study.

Through this guide you can learn data wrangling skills, project workflow and organization, literate programming style, reproducible research techniques, and collaborative analysis development in an open-science context. These skills are learned through R and R-Studio in this guide granted the skills and principles are relevant to many programming languages or analysis softwares available.

The Hakai Institute is a research organization based on the coast of British Columbia, Canada. See the Hakai Institute Website for more information.
